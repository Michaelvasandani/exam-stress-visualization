Mina arrived at the exam hall early. Already, physiological signs of anxiety are apparent:

  • Elevated heart rate: 95 BPM (20% above resting)
  • Increased sweating: EDA readings show rising skin conductance
  • Shallow breathing: Respiratory rate of 18 breaths per minute
  • Slight temperature increase: 0.5Â°C above baseline

"My heart is racing. I keep checking my watch. Did I study enough? What if I blank out on a question?"

These symptoms reflect the body's sympathetic "fight-or-flight" response activating in anticipation of the exam.

9:15 AM

15 Minutes Into the Exam

Heart Rate
110 BPM
Skin Conductance
Very High
Body Temperature
High

As Mina encounters the first difficult question, physiological stress peaks:

  • Heart rate spike: Jumping to 110 BPM
  • EDA surge: Skin conductance reaches highest levels
  • Muscle tension: Accelerometer data shows micro-movements in arms and shoulders
  • Cortisol release: Stress hormone flooding the system

"I can't remember the formula. My mind is blank. Everyone else seems to be writing so much..."

The body's full stress response is now engaged, potentially affecting cognitive performance and memory recall.

10:00 AM

One Hour Into the Exam

Heart Rate
90 BPM
Skin Conductance
Moderate
Body Temperature
Normalizing

Mina has found a rhythm and is beginning to adapt:

  • Heart rate adaptation: Decreased to 90 BPM
  • EDA stabilization: Still elevated but no longer peaking
  • Breathing regulation: More consistent respiratory pattern
  • Reduced muscle tension: Body showing signs of adaptation

"OK, I know this material. Take it one question at a time. Just focus on what's in front of me."

This adaptation phase shows the body's remarkable ability to regulate stress responses even in ongoing challenging situations.

12:05 PM

Just After the Exam

Heart Rate
75 BPM
Skin Conductance
Baseline
Body Temperature
Normal

With the exam complete, Mina's body shows relief:

  • Heart rate normalization: Returning to baseline of 75 BPM
  • EDA decrease: Sharp drop in skin conductance
  • Temperature regulation: Body temperature stabilizing
  • Parasympathetic activation: "Rest and digest" system engaging

"It's over! I think I did OK on most parts. That wasn't as bad as I feared."

The parasympathetic nervous system now activates, countering the stress response and beginning recovery.
